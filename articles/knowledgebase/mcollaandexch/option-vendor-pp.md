# Options for a vendor in the partner portal

**Options for a vendor in the partner portal**  
First, you must give your partner access by sending them an email with an invitation link then your partner needs to accept the invitation through the link in their inbox or junk box and start the process of creating an account.  
After accepting the invitation and registering to the partner portal you have an insight into all partners and which of them accepted the invitation or are still not accepted.

**There are three different types of Partners:**

we distinguish each partner from other partners in which they get access to specific parts of the warehouse Administration.

Here are the options for a Vendor, a vendor can see information about company details like:

**Sales Orders:** A sales order is a document generated by a business to confirm a sale of goods or services to a customer.

It includes details such as the quantity, price, and description of the products or services being sold.

Sales orders are typically created based on a customer’s purchase request and serve as a formal record of the transaction.

in sales orders, you see all sales orders and see whether it is posted or not.

**Warehouse Shipments:** Warehouse shipments refer to the process of sending out goods from a warehouse to fulfill customer orders.

This involves picking the items from inventory, packing them for shipment, and arranging for their delivery to the customer.

Warehouse shipments are an essential part of the order fulfillment process and are crucial for ensuring that customers receive their purchases in a timely manner.

in warehouse shipments, you see all warehouse shipment and see whether it is posted or not.

**Sales Return Orders:**Sales return orders, also known as return merchandise authorizations, are documents used to manage the return of goods from customers.

When a customer wishes to return a product due to issues such as defects or dissatisfaction, a sales return order is created to initiate the return process.

It includes details such as the reason for the return, the condition of the item, and instructions for processing the return.

in sales return orders, you see all sales return orders, and see whether it is posted or not.

**Sales Invoices:** A sales invoice is a document issued by a seller to request payment from a buyer for goods or services that have been provided.

It includes information such as the items sold, their prices, any applicable taxes or discounts, and the total amount due.

Sales invoices serve as a formal request for payment and provide both parties, with a record of the transaction.

in sales invoices, you see all sales invoices and see whether it is posted or not.

**Sales Credit Memos:**A sales credit memo, also known as a credit note, is a document issued by a seller to provide a customer with a credit for returned goods or over payment on an invoice. It serves as evidence of the seller’s obligation to reimburse, the buyer for an amount owed. Sales credit memos are important for maintaining accurate accounting records and ensuring that customers receive appropriate refunds or credits.

in sales credit memos, you see all credit memos and see whether it is posted or not.  
**Exchange profile:**here you can digitally exchange documents, for more information see exchange file automatically tutorial.
